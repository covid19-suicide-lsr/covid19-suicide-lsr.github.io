Full publications of our living systematic review

The latest living review update was published on June 18, 2021 via F1000 research. This updated version replaced the previous versions of this living review: The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on self-harm and suicidal behaviour: update of living systematic review (version 2; peer review: 1 approved, 2 approved with reservations). Please cite as:

Ann John, Emily Eyles, Roger T. Webb, Chukwudi Okolie, Lena Schmidt, Ella Arensman, Keith Hawton, Rory C. O’Connor, Nav Kapur, Paul Moran, Siobhan O’Neill, Luke A. McGuinness, Babatunde K. Our research for Covid-19 and impacts on mental health: publications using data from the living review

We published an analysis of our dataset with respect to suicide in low-and middle-income countries, in the form of a systematic review : Suicide and self-harm in low-and middle-income countries during the COVID-19 pandemic: A systematic review. Please cite as:

Knipe, D., John, A., Padmanathan, P., Eyles, E., Dekel, D., Higgins, J., Bantjes, J., Dandona, R., Macleod-Hall, C., McGuinness, L., Schmidt, L., Webb, R. and Gunnell, D., 2022. Suicide and self-harm in low- and middle- income countries during the COVID-19 pandemic: A systematic review. PLOS Global Public Health, 2(6), p.e0000282.

We published an analysis of our dataset with respect to suicide and SARS-CoV-2 infection, in the form of a systematic review : SARS-CoV-2 infection and the risk of suicidal and self-harm thoughts and behaviour: a systematic review. Please cite as:

Sinyor, M., Zaheer, R., Webb, R., Knipe, D., Eyles, E., Higgins, J., McGuinness, L., Schmidt, L., Macleod-Hall, C., Dekel, D., Gunnell, D. and John, A., 2022. SARS-CoV-2 Infection and the Risk of Suicidal and Self-Harm Thoughts and Behaviour: A Systematic Review. The Canadian Journal of Psychiatry, p.070674372210945.

We published an analysis of our dataset with respect to suicidal behaviour among health and social care workers, in the form of a systematic review : Systematic review of the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on suicidal behaviour amongst health and social care workers across the world. Please cite as:

Eyles, E., Moran, P., Okolie, C., Dekel, D., Macleod-Hall, C., Webb, R., Schmidt, L., Knipe, D., Sinyor, M., McGuinness, L., Arensman, E., Hawton, K., O’Connor, R., Kapur, N., O’Neill, S., Olorisade, B., Cheng, H., Higgins, J., John, A. and Gunnell, D., 2021. Systematic review of the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on suicidal behaviour amongst health and social care workers across the world. Journal of Affective Disorders Reports, 6, p.100271.

We did an analysis of our dataset with respect to self-harm and healthcare service utilisation, in the form of a systematic review. This paper is currently available as pre-print : The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on health service utilisation following self-harm: a systematic review.
